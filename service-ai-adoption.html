The problem we solve

Bought the AI. Now where's the productivity?

By 2026, almost every Fortune 1000 enterprise has bought enterprise AI tooling. Most have rolled out Copilot. Many have stood up internal chat assistants, code-assist tools, agent platforms. The buying decision is largely made.

The harder problem is what comes next: turning purchased AI into measurable productivity gain. Activation rates are high. Daily active usage stays concentrated in a small early-adopter cohort. The rest of the workforce tries it once, finds it useful but not transformative, and reverts to existing workflows. Six months on, the renewal question lands: was this $X million spent well?

Without a systematic value framework — use cases identified, productivity gains measured, change management embedded, governance clearly communicated — the answer is anecdotal. We help organisations make it defensible.

What's included

Six work-streams. One outcome.

Every licence optimisation engagement covers these areas — adapted in scale and depth to your environment, contracts, and timeline.

— 01

Adoption assessment

Where adoption is sticking, where it isn't, and why. Quantitative analysis of usage data, qualitative interviews with target user cohorts, benchmarking against peer organisations using the same tools.

— 02

Use-case prioritisation

Systematic identification and ranking of the highest-value AI use cases — by department, role, and workflow. Each sized for productivity impact, implementation difficulty, and risk.

— 03

Value measurement framework

Methodology for measuring productivity gain in a way that survives executive scrutiny. Metrics design, measurement cadence, baseline-setting protocols, and reporting that connects AI usage to financial outcomes.

— 04

Change-management programme

Training curricula targeted at the use cases that matter, embedded champions in business units, executive communication, ongoing adoption support. We don't try to teach everyone everything — we focus on highest-value learning.

— 05

AI governance & policy design

Acceptable-use policies, data handling guardrails, IP protection rules, model selection guidance, and the governance forum that keeps these alive. Permissive enough to enable adoption, strict enough to manage real risks.

— 06

Portfolio review & rationalisation

Annual review of the AI tool portfolio against measured outcomes. Tools not delivering value are flagged for renegotiation, consolidation, or sunset. Successful tools are scaled. Feeds directly into AI Spend Management.

How we work

A four-stage delivery model. Outcome-anchored.

AI Adoption engagements run alongside the business — typically 16–28 weeks for an initial programme, with quarterly review cadence afterwards.

01

Discover

Free consultation to understand which AI tools you've deployed, what the adoption picture looks like today, and where executive sponsorship is strongest.

02

Assess & design

Adoption assessment, use-case prioritisation, value measurement framework. Typically 4–8 weeks. By the end of this phase, you have a defensible adoption strategy and a measurable value framework.

03

Mobilise

Targeted training programmes, change-management embedding, governance launch. Run alongside business operations over 12–20 weeks. We embed support routines that survive after we leave.

04

Sustain & review

Quarterly value reviews, annual portfolio rationalisation, ongoing adoption analytics. Optional managed service for continued embedding and ongoing portfolio decisions.
