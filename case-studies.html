IBM Enterprise Content Management

From a 2-year stalemate to $100M in 4 months.

Fortune 500 enterprise · IBM ECM estate accumulated over 10–15 years through M&A

Situation

Years of departmental-level IBM purchases combined with mergers and acquisitions had created a tangled set of contractual restrictions. The client's contract and legal teams had engaged directly with IBM for 2–3 years without resolution.

Action

We reviewed every contract, validated every entitlement, interviewed key stakeholders to understand actual usage, then designed a solution that resolved the departmental restriction while optimising licences across the enterprise.

Result

Within four months of formal engagement, the optimisation programme delivered savings exceeding $100 million — a structural fix to a problem that had defeated the client's internal effort for years.

IBM Audit Defence

$40M of audit exposure, settled in 8 weeks.

Leading US multinational investment bank · 300,000+ employees · 330M+ customers worldwide

Situation

The client was being audited by IBM in the area of Enterprise Content Management. Significant non-compliance findings had been raised. After 5 months of unsuccessful internal defence efforts, our team was brought in.

Action

We assessed the environment using our proprietary licensing methodology, compared findings against the auditor's, and quickly identified that the licensing metrics applied diverged from the methodology in the contract. We then engaged IBM directly.

Result

Within 8 weeks of formal engagement, we negotiated over $40 million in immediate, bottom-line cost savings — a structural settlement that closed the audit and restored normal commercial relations with IBM.

Microsoft Baselining

Baselining ahead of an EA true-up — $23M of risk surfaced.

Large North American bank · 12,000+ servers · 67,000+ workstations · decentralised IT & procurement

Situation

The client elected to be proactive ahead of their Microsoft Enterprise Agreement true-up cycle. With completely decentralised IT and procurement — and security concerns about data sharing — they needed a defensible baseline before facing Microsoft.

Action

We reconciled contracts, scanned invoices, Install Base Reports and entitlement data — comparing user requirements per business unit against the available licence pool, despite the client's data-segmentation requirements.

Result

$23M in unnecessary licence risk identified; the client elected to remediate $3M immediately. We also flagged 8 software products critically out of support across 5,000+ installs — driving the next phase of remediation.

Symantec / Veritas

Recurring savings on a multi-business-unit Symantec estate.

Large European automotive manufacturer · 4 business units · multi-region deployment

Situation

The client was concerned its departments were not using Symantec/Veritas licences efficiently and wanted to reduce software spend by optimising assets across all four business units.

Action

We analysed contracts, spreadsheets, scanned invoices, Install Base Reports and entitlement data. User requirements per business unit were identified and compared against the available licence pool to surface waste and tier-mismatches.

Result

Within 8 weeks, we delivered a roadmap recognising over €420,000 in savings. The client subsequently engaged us to manage and optimise their Symantec environment twice a year — converting one project into recurring managed services.

IBM Audit Defence

£1M+ saved in under two weeks during an IBM audit.

Leading intellectual property management company · UK-headquartered · global operations

Situation

The client was being audited by IBM and was unfamiliar with the complex licensing rules at play. The internal team felt overwhelmed and needed specialists to position their case, optimise usage, and lead vendor negotiation.

Action

We scanned the network and inventoried every IBM installation in under a week — accounting for bundling and other licensing mechanics. In parallel, we reconciled software contracts against IBM's data, identified discrepancies, and proposed optimisation techniques.

Result

In under two weeks, more than £1,000,000 was saved from the audit defence work alone. We also designed forward-looking optimisation techniques that would save the client further millions over the following years.

JD Edwards EnterpriseOne

15-year ERP deployment, untangled and optimised.

Large US commercial real estate management firm · multiple subsidiaries & affiliates

Situation

A 15-year-old JD Edwards EnterpriseOne deployment had become unclear in terms of consumption and cost allocation, following years of acquisitions and divestitures. The client wanted to review software consumption and rebuild controls.

Action

We performed a deep-dive analysis of every historic agreement, leveraging deep knowledge of contract evolution. We then analysed software consumption across subsidiaries and affiliates using customised tools, surfacing future savings and a tracking framework.

Result

$1.5M of financial risk identified from inadvertent unauthorised use — proactively remediated through licence renegotiation. A further $0.4M in recurring annual savings unlocked through harvesting unused entitlements, plus a new cost-allocation tracking mechanism.

IBM Maximo

Optimising Maximo across 5,000+ users in a multi-vertical utility.

Multinational utility company · 150,000+ employees · 5,000+ Maximo users · electricity, gas, renewables

Situation

The client engaged us to proactively review their Maximo Asset Management deployment — their primary asset management solution — and identify optimisation opportunities across an estate that was integrated with their ERP and many third-party tools.

Action

We reverse-engineered the deployment and its integration footprint, then analysed Maximo deployments through a variety of consumption reports — surfacing several issues in access configuration and third-party integration.

Result

Within 3 months, a detailed roadmap delivered for over $4.5M in savings — driven by remediating improper access configuration and tightening controls over third-party software integration touchpoints.
